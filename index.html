WELCOME TO CHITRADURGA

About

Chitradurga Fort, also known as "Kallina Kote" (Fort of Stone), is one of the most magnificent historical forts in Karnataka, India. Located in the rocky hills of Chitradurga district, it is famous for its unique architecture, strategic design, and natural defense system.

Built between the 11th and 18th centuries, the fort was ruled by the Chalukyas, Hoysalas, Nayakas of Chitradurga, Hyder Ali, Tipu Sultan, and later the British.

The fort is renowned for its seven concentric walls, 19 gateways, 38 posterior entrances, 35 secret entrances, and 4,000 watchtowers. These features made it nearly impossible for enemies to invade.

It is also associated with legends from the Mahabharata and the bravery of Onake Obavva, who defended the fort with a pestle. Today, it stands as a symbol of bravery, heritage, and architectural excellence.

Places Inside the Fort

  1. Hidimbeshwara Temple – Oldest temple inside the fort, linked to Mahabharata.
  2. Ekanatheshwari Temple – Shrine dedicated to goddess worshipped by Nayakas.
  3. Sampige Siddeshwara Temple – Major temple inside the fort.
  4. Onake Obavvana Kindi – Narrow passage where Obavva defended the fort.
  5. Water Reservoirs & Tanks – Example: Akka-Tangi Honda (Tank of Two Sisters).

Architecture of Chitradurga Fort

  1. Seven Concentric Walls – Strong defense made of granite boulders.
  2. 19 Massive Gates – Strategically placed to slow invaders.
  3. 4,000+ Watchtowers – For long-distance surveillance.
  4. Secret Passages – Hidden paths for escape and movement.
  5. Temples & Mathas – Blended into the fort’s layout.
  6. Kudure Kallu (Horse Rock) – Natural rock formation resembling a horse.
